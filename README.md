# Supply Chain Analysis of Makeup Products

This analysis explores a dataset containing various aspects of a makeup product supply chain, including product information, sales data, customer demographics, stock levels, logistics, manufacturing details, and defect rates. The primary goal is to identify key trends, efficiencies, and areas for improvement within the supply chain.

## Dataset Overview

The dataset consists of 100 entries with 24 columns, covering details such as `Product type`, `Price`, `Availability`, `Number of products sold`, `Revenue generated`, `Shipping carriers`, `Manufacturing costs`, and `Defect rates`.

## Key Findings and Insights

### 1. Sales and Revenue by Product Type
*   **Skincare products** are the leading revenue generators, accounting for approximately **45%** of the total sales, followed by haircare (29.5%) and cosmetics (25.5%).
*   There is a positive correlation between `Price` and `Revenue generated`, with higher-priced products generally generating more revenue.

To analyze sales by product type, the following code was used:
```python
sales_data = df.groupby('Product type')['Number of products sold'].sum().reset_index()
pie_chart = px.pie(sales_data,values='Number of products sold',
                   title='Sales by Product Type',
                   hover_data=['Number of products sold'],
                   names='Product type',
                   hole=0.4,
                   color_discrete_sequence=px.colors.qualitative.Pastel)
pie_chart.update_traces(textposition='inside',textinfo = 'percent+label')
pie_chart.show()
```

### 2. Shipping Carrier Performance
*   **Carrier B** is responsible for generating the most revenue for the company, indicating its significant role in product distribution.
*   Coincidentally, **Carrier B** also incurs higher shipping costs, likely due to a larger volume of orders and associated transportation.

The revenue generated by each shipping carrier was calculated using:
```python
total_revenue = df.groupby('Shipping carriers')['Revenue generated'].sum().reset_index()
```

### 3. Manufacturing and Logistics Efficiency
*   **Cosmetics** have the lowest average lead time (13.5 days) and average manufacturing costs (43.05), suggesting a more efficient production and delivery process compared to haircare and skincare.
*   **Haircare products** exhibit the highest average lead time (18.7 days) and manufacturing costs (48.46).

Average lead times and manufacturing costs were determined as follows:
```python
avg_lead_time = df.groupby('Product type')['Lead time'].mean().reset_index()
avg_manufacturing_cost = df.groupby('Product type')['Manufacturing costs'].mean().reset_index()

result = pd.merge(avg_lead_time,avg_manufacturing_cost,on ='Product type')
result.rename(columns={'Lead time':'Average Lead Time','Manufacturing costs':'Average Manufacturing Costs'},inplace= True)
print(result)
```

### 5. Transportation Modes and Costs
*   The company significantly spends on **Road** and **Rail** modes of transportation.
*   **Road transportation** also accounts for the highest percentage of total defect rates, indicating potential issues with this mode that need further investigation.
*   **Air transportation** has the lowest defect rate, suggesting it's the safest option for product delivery, though it might be more costly.

Cost distribution by transportation mode was visualized with:
```python
transportation_chart = px.pie(df,values='Costs',
```

### 6. Defect Rates
*   **Haircare products** have a higher average defect rate compared to skincare and cosmetics. This suggests a need to investigate the manufacturing or handling processes specific to haircare.
*   Defect rates vary significantly by transportation mode, with Road transportation contributing the most to product defects.

Defect rates by transportation mode were analyzed using:
```python
pivot_table = pd.pivot_table(df,values='Defect rates',
                             index=['Transportation modes'],
                             aggfunc='mean')
transportation_chart = px.pie(values=pivot_table['Defect rates'],
                              names=pivot_table.index,
                              title='Defect Rates by Transportation Mode',
                              hole=0.5,
                              color_discrete_sequence=px.colors.qualitative.Pastel)
transportation_chart.show()
```

## Recommendations

1.  **Optimize Haircare Supply Chain**: Investigate the causes behind the higher defect rates and longer lead times for haircare products. This could involve reviewing manufacturing processes, packaging, or handling during transportation.
2.  **Carrier Performance Review**: While Carrier B generates high revenue, analyze its cost-efficiency considering its higher shipping costs. Explore opportunities to negotiate better rates or diversify carriers if costs are disproportionately high.
3.  **Transportation Mode Optimization**: Re-evaluate the use of Road transportation, especially for sensitive products. While cost-effective, its higher defect rate might lead to overall losses. Consider shifting high-value or fragile items to Air or Sea if feasible.
4.  **Inventory Management**: Use SKU-level insights to optimize stock levels and order quantities, reducing holding costs and preventing stockouts.
5.  **Focus on Skincare**: Continue leveraging the strong performance of skincare products and explore strategies to further boost their sales and market penetration.
